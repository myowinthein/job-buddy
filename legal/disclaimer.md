# Disclaimer — Job Buddy

**Effective date:** June 27, 2026

## Nature of the tool

Job Buddy is an autofill convenience tool. It reads information from a
profile you create and attempts to fill corresponding fields on job
application forms. It is not a recruitment service, career advisor,
employment agency, or professional advisory service of any kind.

## No guarantee of accuracy

Job Buddy's field-matching is based on automated heuristics — HTML
attributes, label text, dictionary matching, and fuzzy string comparison.
This process is imperfect. Job Buddy may:

- Fill a field with the wrong profile value.
- Fail to fill a field it should have filled.
- Miss fields that are hidden, dynamically loaded, or built with
  non-standard components.

**You must review every field Job Buddy fills before submitting any
application.** Job Buddy never submits a form on your behalf, but it is
your responsibility to verify the content before you do.

## AI-generated content

Job Buddy includes optional AI-assisted features (Resume Import and
AI Autofill assist) that use Google's Gemini API via an API key you
supply. AI-generated suggestions — including fields extracted from your
résumé and field-match suggestions during autofill — are produced by a
large language model and may be inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, or
inappropriate.

- AI suggestions are a starting point, not a verified output.
- Job Buddy does not independently verify any value produced by the AI.
- You are solely responsible for reviewing, correcting, and accepting or
  rejecting every AI-generated suggestion before it is saved to your
  profile or submitted on a form.

## No employment advice

Job Buddy does not provide advice on how to complete job applications,
what information to disclose, how to present your experience, or any
other aspect of the job-seeking process. Nothing produced by Job Buddy —
including AI-generated extractions or autofill suggestions — constitutes
career, legal, or employment advice.

## Special category data

Job Buddy's profile includes optional fields for gender, ethnicity,
veteran status, and disability status. These fields exist because many
job application forms include equivalent equal-opportunity employment
questions. You are solely responsible for deciding whether and how to
complete these fields on any application, and for understanding the
implications of disclosing this information in the jurisdiction and context
in which you are applying.
